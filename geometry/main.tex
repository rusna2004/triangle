1.5.4 Find the distance from $\vec{I}$ to $BC$.

\solution : 
We know the value of $\vec{I}$ is
\begin{align}
\vec{I} &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{37} + 4 + \sqrt{61}} \myvec{\sqrt{61} - 16 - 3\sqrt{37}\\ -\sqrt{61} + 24 - 5\sqrt{37}}
\end{align}
from the problem 1.5.2 .
The equation of $BC$ is:
\begin{align}
\myvec{11&1}\vec{x} + 38=0
\end{align}

Let $r$ be the distance between $\vec{I}$ and $BC$, then
\begin{align}
r &= \frac{\abs{\myvec{11&1} \vec{I} + 38}}{\norm{\myvec{11 \\ 1}}} \\
&= \frac{\abs{\frac{1}{\sqrt{37} + 4 + \sqrt{61}} \myvec{11&1} \myvec{\sqrt{61} - 16 - 3\sqrt{37}\\ -\sqrt{61} + 24 - 5\sqrt{37}} + 38}}{\sqrt{{11}^2 + {1}^2}} \\
&= \frac{\frac{10\sqrt{61} - 38\sqrt{37} - 152}{\sqrt{37} + 4 + \sqrt{61}} + 38}{\sqrt{122}} \\
&= \frac{48\sqrt{61}}{\sqrt{122} {(\sqrt{37} + 4 + \sqrt{61}})} \\
&= \frac{48}{\sqrt{2}(\sqrt{37} + 4 + \sqrt{61})}\\
&= \frac{24\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{37}+4+\sqrt{61}}\\
&= 1.8969                                        
\end{align}
